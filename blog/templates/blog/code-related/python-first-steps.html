Python Guide: First Steps

Starting out with a new (maybe even first?) programming language is a serious step forward. Don't worry though, using this guide will make the whole process waaay easier and faster. Let's go! Here is what our plan looks like:

  • Basic Syntax
  • Variables and types
  • Lists (Kind of like arrays)
  • Basic operators
  • String formatting
  • Loops

Let's begin! Python is a pretty simple language with a very "human-readable" and straightforward syntax. Most of the words you will see mean exactly the thing they mean in English.

That should be it for now. If you want to continue learning, I suggest you to look at some of the project ideas I have on the website. From there on, just think of a simple project, start working on it and literally Google every single line of code you need. Through repetition and exploration you learn the basics, while thinking of a project architecture as a whole makes you better at the most important thing - thinking like a programmer, breaking down complex projects into blocks of very simple problems which can be easily tackled one by one. Once broken down, you just need to Google a sequence of one-liners, like "python write to csv" or "python 3 importing modules". Trust me, even experienced programmers sometimes spend most of their day just looking up stuff online, so learning to properly formulate your search queries is essential!

Overall, the whole journey to becoming a Python pro is massively overwhelming, but don't worry - with enough patience, this is going to be a walk in the park! Albeit, a quite long one...
