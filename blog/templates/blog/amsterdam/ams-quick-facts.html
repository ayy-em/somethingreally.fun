Must know facts about The Netherlands and Amsterdam

Definitely handy to know these prior to traveling here. They also can be entertaining enough to keep you from going to sleep for five more minutes, which you would totally regret the next morning. Anyway, let's get this started! You can click the facts to learn more. Well, you should, but I did not test it on any browsers other than Chrome, so good luck.

What other basics do you want to know? Amsterdam is the third busiest airport and fifth largest port in Europe, a cultural capital of the Netherlands and its 17th century canals are on the UNESCO World Heritage List.

Which basically means "We built a bridge and a dam across the river Amstel and we're gonna live here". It got city rights in either 1300 or 1306. Not that I don't remember, it's just that there are multiple opinions on the topic.

At a stunning 183,9 cm on average for males, the Netherlands is either the tallest nation or shares it with Bosnia and Herzegovina, Montenegro and Denmark, claims this random article.

Even though Dutch is obviously the official language in Amsterdam, most of the residents speak English as well as some natives do. Wiki mentions 93% of Dutch people claim to be able to converse freely in English. The share of bilinguals is even higher in the capital and the Dutch version of English even has a name - Dunglish (or steenkolenengels).
