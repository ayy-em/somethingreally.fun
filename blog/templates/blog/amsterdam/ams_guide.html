So how do I get there?

Getting to Amsterdam is easy - the city itself is quite small and the public transportation system is spot on. When you say "get to Amsterdam", it's implied that you need to get to Amsterdam Centraal (also called "Amsterdam CS" sometimes), the train station in the heart of the city. There are basically a few options of how to get there:

  • Plane. There are actually six international airports in the Netherlands, however, 95% of air traffic comes from either Amsterdam Airport Schiphol (AMS) or the Eindhoven Airport (EIN). Getting to Amsterdam Centraal from Schiphol is super easy - you can either take an airport shuttle bus (€6,50 - departs every 10 minutes and takes about 25 minutes), a taxi (~ €30 on average for a 20-minute ride) or a train (€4,30 for a 15-minute ride - by far, the fastest/cheapest option).
  • Train. Most of the trains go directly to Amsterdam Centraal.
  • Bus. The usual European bus companies like FlixBus mostly arrive at the Amsterdam Sloterdijk station. From there, you can either take a train (runs every 10 minutes, takes less than 5 to reach CS) or hop in the metro station (takes about 25 minutes as it goes all the way around the city).
  • Car. The big issue here is parking. P+R car park is a great option for a short stay (96 hours or less). Otherwise, you'd need to google something else.

Once you're in the city centre, it's time to do something really fun!

Where do I even stay?

Airbnb is the go-to. Prices are pretty insane, that's for sure. Off-season, a super-small apartment outside the city centre would cost you around €80 a night. Staying in the city centre during, say, Christmas season or King's day can easily cost you around €250 a night! It is insanity, yes, we're fully aware. Oh, by the way, in case you've never used Airbnb before, here's an invite link from me - use it to get €20 off your first trip.

Pretty much the same can be said about hotels. Not only is the availability limited, the quality of service also sucks. Tiny rooms with no windows you can hear your neighbours whisper in for €189 a night are a sad reality we live in.

Other than that, I suggest you look through Facebook and try to find a short-stay room there - sometimes you get lucky and see a student leave the city for a week to go home or an expat willing to let others in to not be as sad about having his €1'800 rent go to waste while he's on vacation. Be careful though, as these groups are filled to the brim with scammers - never send a payment in advance!
