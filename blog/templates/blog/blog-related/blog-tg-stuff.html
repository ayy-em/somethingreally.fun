Telegram Stuff

I got really into Telegram bots at some point - you can check these out in the cards below. They're all pretty simple and the code is usually available on Github so you can easily build one yourself. Why do I like these bots so much? Well, they're easy to get up and running, you can use any language you're comfortable with (especially if you read my guide) and once you make one, making the second one is so easy because you know your shit. Anyway, here's some stuff I do on/with Telegram.

Amsterdam #Live

As far as channels go, this is my biggest one - I even have a page about it. Photos, videos, funny stories and tips about Amsterdam. Russian only, unfortunately.

@adam24live

Vice News

Not a huge fan myself, but my girlfriend likes it, so I made a bot that posts the most interesting articles on Vice every day at 13:00 Amsterdam time. English only, obviously.

@vice_news

Cool Maps

If you're as obsessed with neat cartography and useless data as I am, subscribe to my Interesting Maps channel. The channel is in Russian but the maps themselves are in English most of the time.

@ayy_maps

I also have quite a few bots running there:

Jack's Helper Bot

Personal assistant bot I describe in detail here. Reminders, weather forecasts, a few phrases here and there... Pretty cool bot that also posts Vice to the channel. Also sends me my favorite cafe's menu and spits out amazing facts!

@JacksHelperBot

Making Telegram bots is pretty easy and really fun! You can automate a lot of recurring tasks using them, streamline your comm tasks and do a lot of other exciting things using them as a makeshift interface of sorts.
